<product_lens>
## Product lens (why this phase exists)

KitsuBeat keeps a TV/Full toggle on every song. The TV cut is the recognizable anime opening (~1:30 vs full 4:00) — high engagement, lower commitment, plays the YouTube embed with anime visuals. Lesson **content** is identical between TV and Full (TV is a re-timed subset of full's verses), so this phase is not about content quality. It's about **timing precision**: the verse the user is watching highlight at the right TV-audio moment.

A broken TV lesson today (5 verses, 38-second gaps, wrong-language verse during JP vocals) is a streak-killer for users who landed on a TV-default song. Same churn risk as a 404 video.
</product_lens>

### Why 10b is broken on cleaner input

Demonstrated empirically on sign-flow (2026-04-26):
- yt-dlp + htdemucs + WhisperX large-v3 ja produced a clean 4-segment TV transcript with avg confidence 0.77.
- Re-running 10b-derive-tv-lessons against this cleaner input still returns `[no_detected_verses]` at every threshold (0.4, 0.2, 0.1).
- Cause: per-verse romaji LCS finds 90-100% char matches scattered across the entire 457-char TV transcript. `MAX_SPAN_RATIO=2.0` rejects every verse because the matched-char span (~450 chars) is 15-20× the verse length (~25 chars). This is a fundamental algorithm-vs-input-quality problem, not a tunable threshold.

## Solution shape

### 1. New TV Demucs pipeline (mirrors full-version A/B)

| Step | Input | Output | Tooling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Download | TV `youtube_id` from `song_versions` | `data/audio-tv/{slug}.mp3` | yt-dlp |
| Stem isolate | `data/audio-tv/{slug}.mp3` | `data/vocal-stems-tv/htdemucs/{slug}/vocals.wav` | htdemucs (CPU 0.3× realtime) |
| Transcribe | stem | `data/timing-cache-tv-stem/{slug}.json` (slug-keyed schema matching `data/timing-cache-tv/`) | whisperx large-v3 ja, `PYTHONIOENCODING=utf-8` mandatory |

Mirror [scripts/seed/ab-demucs-isolate.py](../../../scripts/seed/ab-demucs-isolate.py) and [scripts/seed/ab-transcribe-stems.py](../../../scripts/seed/ab-transcribe-stems.py) — same shape, TV paths.

### 2. New alignment core: Needleman-Wunsch global alignment

Replace [scripts/seed/10b-derive-tv-lessons.ts](../../../scripts/seed/10b-derive-tv-lessons.ts) with a fresh derive:

- **Inputs**: full-lesson romaji concatenated verse-by-verse with verse-boundary markers + TV stem WhisperX romaji.
- **Algorithm**: NW with phoneme-blind char scoring. Starting parameters: match=+2, mismatch=-1, gap=-1. Tunable per Japanese vowel/consonant class if needed (e.g., vowel-vowel mismatch -0.5, consonant mismatch -1.5).
- **Output per verse**: matched-char count + char range in TV. Threshold for "verse present in TV": ≥40% matched-chars within verse range.
- **Verse timing**: `tv_start = TV time of first matched char in verse range`, `tv_end = TV time of last matched char`. Then apply gap-midpoint expansion (10b's existing logic) for tight inter-verse boundaries.

Why NW over LCS / Smith-Waterman / windowed-LCS:
- LCS scatters across the whole TV — the bug we're fixing.
- Smith-Waterman is local (best contiguous substring) — wrong fit; we need full-coverage alignment, not best-local-match.
- Windowed LCS (tried in [10b2-derive-tv-monotonic.ts](../../../scripts/seed/10b2-derive-tv-monotonic.ts)) can't anchor against repeated romaji phonemes.
- NW is monotonic by construction → no scatter, no `MAX_SPAN_RATIO` hacks.

O(N×M) memory: ~5K full chars × ~500 TV chars = 2.5M cells. Fine.
