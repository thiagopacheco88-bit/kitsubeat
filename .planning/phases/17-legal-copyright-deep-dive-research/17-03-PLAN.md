**§3.1 Consumer Law — Distance Selling & Digital Content**

Four sub-subsections:

- **§3.1.1 UK Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 (CCRs)**
  - Applicability: B2C distance sales, kitsubeat is IN SCOPE for any UK consumer purchase
  - 14-day cancellation right (Reg. 29): scope, the "right to cancel" notice obligation (must be given pre-contract), the 14-day clock starts on purchase for digital content
  - **Digital content waiver mechanics** (Reg. 37): consumer can consent to immediate supply (starting to use the service immediately), but MUST acknowledge that doing so extinguishes the 14-day cancel right — the waiver must be: (i) express, (ii) evidenced, (iii) informed. This is the single most important consumer-law requirement for a digital subscription.
  - Specific wording requirements Phase 18 implements: the "cancel" wording, the "waiver" wording, the combined checkout copy for a subscription that starts immediately
  - Cite the regulation by SI number (SI 2013/3134), link to legislation.gov.uk, link to CMA / Which? guidance
  - Emit `REQ-CONS-UK-NN` requirement IDs for each obligation

- **§3.1.2 EU Consumer Rights Directive (Directive 2011/83/EU as amended by Directive 2019/2161 "Modernisation Directive")**
  - Parallel structure to §3.1.1 — the EU regime mirrors the UK on the 14-day window and digital-content waiver but with minor wording and evidence differences per Art. 16(m)
  - Note transposition fragmentation: each member state transposes the Directive into national law; kitsubeat cannot publish ONE "EU" T&Cs — the minimum-harmonisation baseline is what the template targets
  - Cite the Directive articles (Art. 9 cancellation right, Art. 14 diligence of return, Art. 16(m) digital-content waiver)
  - Emit `REQ-CONS-EU-NN` IDs

- **§3.1.3 Brazil CDC (Código de Defesa do Consumidor, Lei 8.078/1990) — brief**
  - The Brazilian audience is material (CONTEXT). CDC has a 7-day cancel right (Art. 49) for off-premises purchases. Digital subscriptions — applicability is contested. Short paragraph + lawyer-flag.
  - Emit `REQ-CONS-BR-NN` + `🚩 LAWYER-REQUIRED {#lawyer-cons-01}` on the CDC digital-subscription scope question.

- **§3.1.4 CCPA-state consumer-rights overlap**
  - California does not have a distance-selling 14-day cancel equivalent for digital subscriptions; Automatic Renewal Law (ARL — Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code §17600) applies to auto-renewing subscriptions — this IS in scope at monetization.
  - Short subsection covering ARL disclosure-at-signup and cancel-path requirements ("clear and conspicuous" cancel mechanism, acknowledgement email).
  - Emit `REQ-CONS-CA-NN` IDs.
