**§5.1 AADC Applicability to kitsubeat**
- ICO Age Appropriate Design Code (AADC / "Children's Code") is statutory guidance under DPA 2018 s.123 — binding on UK online services "likely to be accessed by children" (under 18)
- Applicability test from the Code: (i) target audience young? (ii) actual audience includes children? (iii) service features appeal to children? kitsubeat: anime music + Japanese = yes on all three. In scope.
- Applicability beyond UK: ICO can enforce UK-GDPR on processing of UK minors' data. EU equivalents (GDPR Art. 8 for children's consent at 16, lowered to 13 by several member states) layer on top.
- Cite the Code: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/childrens-information/childrens-code-guidance-and-resources/age-appropriate-design-a-code-of-practice-for-online-services/

**§5.2 AADC 15 Standards — Per-Standard Analysis**

Produce a dedicated sub-subsection for each of the 15 standards. Each sub-subsection is short (one paragraph + bullet obligations):

1. **Best interests of the child** — kitsubeat's design choices (gamification, streaks, sound/haptic celebrations) must not exploit children. Specific obligation: streaks DO NOT use loss-aversion dark patterns (already aligned per Phase 12 CONTEXT's "one auto-applied grace day per week"); confetti/haptic is configurable (already in Phase 12 success criteria).
2. **Data protection impact assessments (DPIAs)** — MUST complete a DPIA before launch processing children's data. Cite DPA 2018 s.57 + ICO DPIA guidance. This is a Phase 18 gate.
3. **Age-appropriate application** — design the experience appropriate to the user's age. Options: (a) design for the youngest likely user (13+) OR (b) implement age bands. AADC allows either. kitsubeat picks (a) for v1 since the product has NO age-inappropriate content (no chat, no UGC, no ads, no messaging).
4. **Transparency** — privacy info explained in language children can understand. Phase 18 obligation: publish a kid-friendly version of the Privacy Policy alongside the adult version.
5. **Detrimental use of data** — do not process children's data in ways ICO evidence shows harm children. Obligation: no behavioural advertising based on minors' learning patterns; analytics respect the minor-default.
6. **Policies and community standards** — respect published policies. Obligation: T&Cs + Privacy + Community standards apply to kitsubeat uniformly (no quiet deviation from published policies for minor accounts).
7. **Default settings** — HIGH-PRIVACY DEFAULTS for children. Critical obligation, expanded in §5.4.
8. **Data minimisation** — collect only what's necessary. Critical obligation, expanded in §5.4.
9. **Data sharing** — do not disclose children's data unless compelling reason. Obligation: no data-sharing with third-party advertisers; Sentry/PostHog event payloads for minors scrub any non-essential identifier.
10. **Geolocation** — geolocation OFF by default for children; switch returns to off after each session. kitsubeat uses timezone (Phase 12), NOT geolocation — confirm this is explicit in the Privacy Policy.
11. **Parental controls** — if provided, clearly signaled to the child; obvious sign when monitored. kitsubeat has NO parental-controls features in v1 — document this as a "not applicable" with rationale and a re-check trigger (if family plans ever ship).
12. **Profiling** — profiling OFF by default for children. FSRS-driven review-queue personalization is "profiling" in the strict sense but is the core learning function — ICO explicitly allows profiling essential to the service. Obligation: publish a justification in Privacy Policy.
13. **Nudge techniques** — do not use nudges that lead children to weaken privacy or engage unhealthily. Obligation: gamification (streaks, level-ups) MUST have configurable sound/haptic per Phase 12 success criterion 4; streak "save your streak" prompts MUST NOT use dark-pattern urgency (already aligned).
14. **Connected toys and devices** — N/A (kitsubeat is web, no IoT).
15. **Online tools** — provide tools for children to exercise their data rights. Obligation: a child-accessible data-export/erasure path in Phase 18's DSAR implementation.

**§5.3 Signup Gate — 13+ with Parental-Awareness Flow**

Concrete UX spec:

1. **Signup form change:** Add a required `date_of_birth` field to the signup form. Store DOB (not just age) so the system can re-evaluate as the user ages into adult status on their 18th birthday.
2. **Client-side evaluation rules:**
   - If DOB → age < 13: block signup with the message "kitsubeat isn't available for under-13s. This helps keep our community safe. You can come back on your 13th birthday." Do NOT store the attempted email address beyond the rate-limit short-term log.
   - If 13 ≤ age < 18: proceed to the "teen signup awareness" step (§5.3.3). Do NOT require parental consent under UK-GDPR (age of digital consent in UK is 13, so 13+ can consent for themselves for information-society services under UK-GDPR Art. 8 as transposed by DPA 2018 s.9). EU member states vary: several (FR, DE) set age of digital consent at 16. Since kitsubeat is GLOBAL, the safest posture is 13+ self-consent under UK-GDPR + a parallel parental-awareness flow that covers the EU 16+ member states non-statutorily.
   - If age ≥ 18: proceed to standard signup.
3. **Teen signup awareness step (§5.3.3):**
   - Copy: "You're under 18. Here's what you should know:" followed by a 4–6 bullet child-friendly privacy summary (what data we collect, that the account defaults to private, that they can delete anytime via profile, that no ads, that sessions may track learning progress).
   - Single acknowledgment checkbox: "I understand."
   - Parental-awareness nudge: "Want to share this with a parent/guardian? [Copy link] or [Email a summary]" — optional button that generates a pre-filled mailto: or a sharable URL. NOT a parental-consent gate.
4. **Spoof-resistance:** No strong age verification. Rationale: per AADC Standard 3, proportionate measures only — a DOB + acknowledgment is proportionate for a low-risk learning service. If the service ever adds high-risk features (chat, UGC), re-evaluate (lawyer-flag this trigger).
5. **Repeat-signup prevention:** if a user fails the <13 gate, do NOT session-cookie them such that they can retry with a different DOB. Use the short-lived attempted-email rate limiter as a soft deterrent. The AADC does NOT require hard verification.
6. **18th-birthday transition:** on their 18th birthday, send an email advising that the account has transitioned to adult defaults and point to the settings page to review visibility / sharing preferences. This is an AADC Standard 7 (default settings) continuity obligation.

**§5.5 LGPD + CCPA Minor Interactions**

- **LGPD Art. 14:** processing children's/adolescents' data requires specific, highlighted, informed consent; consent from at least one parent/guardian required for children (under 12 in Brazilian law). Adolescents (12–17) can consent for themselves in many contexts but with data minimization.

- Applicability to kitsubeat: Brazilian signups from ages 13–17 require data minimization + highlighted consent. Under-13 Brazilian signups require parental consent — kitsubeat's 13+ gate blocks these, satisfying both LGPD and AADC.
