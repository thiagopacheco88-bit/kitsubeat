**§4.1 EU AI Act — Applicability & Enforcement Timeline**

- Cite Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 ("AI Act") by its OJ reference.
- Applicability: kitsubeat is a downstream "deployer" of GPAI (General-Purpose AI) and an "provider" of AI-enabled output if it packages Claude output into a distinct service. Both roles create obligations.
- Staged enforcement timeline (cite Art. 113):
  - 2024-08-01: Regulation enters into force
  - 2025-02-02: Prohibited-practice provisions + AI-literacy obligations apply
  - 2025-08-02: GPAI provider obligations + governance provisions apply
  - 2026-08-02: Most obligations apply (including Art. 50 transparency for deployers)
  - 2027-08-02: High-risk AI system provisions fully apply
- Map these milestones to kitsubeat's v3.0 launch window (2026) and v4.0 window:
  - At v3.0 launch: Art. 50 transparency IS live → Phase 18 MUST implement disclosure
  - AI literacy obligations (Art. 4) are live → kitsubeat MUST ensure sufficient AI literacy among staff who operate AI systems (for a solo operator, this is documented-in-SOPs)
- Table: Obligation | Article | Effective date | kitsubeat applicability | Phase assignment

**§4.2 Art. 50 Transparency — WhisperX (AI-generated text output)**

- Art. 50(2): deployers of AI systems that generate or manipulate text/image/audio/video "which is made public" must disclose that the content has been artificially generated or manipulated.
- Exception: Art. 50(2) final paragraph exempts "assistive function for standard editing" and cases where "the AI-generated content has undergone a process of human review or editorial control." — WhisperX output IS edited by a subsequent pipeline step (timing editor, verse coverage QA). Analyze whether this exception applies and rate.
- If exception applies (likely partial): kitsubeat still MUST label the transcripts as AI-generated since the edit is mechanical, not editorial.
- Disclosure placement: research where — inline metadata? badge in UI? About page? Privacy/AI Policy? — and propose the minimum-viable compliant UX pattern for Phase 18.
- Machine-readable disclosure (Art. 50(2) requires "in a marked format … detectable as artificially generated or manipulated") — research whether a `data-ai-generated="true"` DOM attribute or embedded C2PA-style metadata is required or whether visible labeling suffices. Confirm with EDPB or EU AI Office guidance when available; where guidance is not yet published, flag as `🚩 LAWYER-REQUIRED {#lawyer-ai-01}`.
- Emit `REQ-AI-WHISPER-NN` IDs.

**§4.3 Art. 50 Transparency — Claude-generated lesson content**

- Cover the full Claude-generated surface: vocab entries (meaning/part-of-speech), grammar explanations, verse-level translations, verse-level natural-sounding explanations, mnemonics (Phase 08.3), kanji breakdowns (Phase 08.3), LearnCard content (Phase 08.4), exercise distractors and explanations (Phase 8, 10).
- Same Art. 50 analysis as §4.2 — but with a crucial difference: lesson content is edited through the Verse Coverage Agent (Phase 1) + human review checkpoints → the "human review or editorial control" exception applies more cleanly here than to WhisperX.
- HOWEVER: the scale of Claude-generated content means disclosure is still the defensible posture. Propose:
  - A global disclosure in the Privacy / About copy: "Lesson explanations, translations, and vocabulary notes are initially drafted by an LLM (Claude), then verified by the Verse Coverage Agent."
  - A per-lesson visible indicator (subtle "AI-assisted" footer on the lesson panel)
  - A TL;DR in the cookie/consent banner covering that AI is used
- Emit `REQ-AI-LESSON-NN` IDs.

**§4.4 AI Act — training-data transparency + other live obligations**

- Rating: 🟡 for the Claude disclosure surface (implement with mitigation), 🟡 for WhisperX (same + machine-readable metadata question), 🟢 for the "not high-risk" classification subject to re-check if kitsubeat ever issues formal certifications.
