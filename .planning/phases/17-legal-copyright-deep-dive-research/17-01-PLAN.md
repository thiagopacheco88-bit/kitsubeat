**1.2 Lyrics Licensing (deepest subsection per CONTEXT.md)**
Break into four sub-subsections:

- **1.2.1 LRCLIB posture** — Document what LRCLIB is (open, user-contributed lyric database), their stated legal position (pull their about/FAQ/ToS page), the copyright status of user-submitted lyrics generally (user uploads do not grant kitsubeat a license to the underlying composition), and how kitsubeat currently uses LRCLIB data. Short — summary + link per CONTEXT.
- **1.2.2 Musixmatch v. Genius precedent deep-dive** — This is the required "highest-risk precedent walk." Cover:
  - The map/trap-street detection methodology (alternating apostrophe styles in lyrics to fingerprint copying)
  - Why the case was ultimately dismissed on preemption grounds (copyright preemption of state-law breach-of-contract claims), not on whether scraping lyrics was lawful
  - The distinction between (a) lyrics as copyrighted musical compositions (publisher rights, ASCAP/BMI/JASRAC for Japanese works) vs (b) synced lyric timing data (the arrangement/database, contested)
  - Canonical citation: ML Genius Holdings LLC v. Google LLC, No. 20-3113 (2d Cir. 2022) — include the court, docket, and a publicly accessible summary URL (EFF, Reuters, Bloomberg Law, or Justia all acceptable)
  - Application to kitsubeat: we are NOT scraping; we use LRCLIB (user-contributed). But we DO re-time lyrics via WhisperX against the original audio, which creates a new synced dataset — risk surface per 1.2.3.
- **1.2.3 WhisperX re-transcription as derivative work** — The interesting edge case per CONTEXT. Analyze:
  - Under US copyright law (17 U.S.C. § 101 definition of "derivative work") and UK CDPA 1988 equivalents, does a ML-generated transcript of a copyrighted song constitute a derivative work?
  - Distinguish: (a) the audio recording's sound-recording copyright (held by label), (b) the underlying musical composition copyright (held by publisher / managed by JASRAC for Japanese anime songs), (c) the lyrics copyright (composition-bundled or separate depending on jurisdiction).
  - Is a time-aligned transcript a "reproduction" or a "derivative"? Cite academic / practitioner commentary (e.g. Copyright Alliance, EFF lyric-site explainers, UK IPO guidance on computer-generated works under CDPA s.9(3)).
  - JASRAC's aggressive enforcement posture for Japanese music rights — cite JASRAC's public lyric-display licensing tariffs if findable.
  - Risk rating + 🚩 LAWYER-REQUIRED marker if the derivative-work exposure cannot be cleanly mitigated with a takedown process.
- **1.2.4 Synced-lyric / karaoke precedent** — Summary of relevant cases (any Musixmatch v. third-party takedown actions, any published karaoke-licensing litigation). Shorter than 1.2.2. Cite where possible.
